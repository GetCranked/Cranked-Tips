CRANKED: TIPS & TRICKS

Note: Not all of these are confirmed mechanics—some could be subtle or even placebo effects. Test everything yourself and adapt to what works for you. This guide incorporates community feedback; techniques may vary by player and situation.

CORE PHILOSOPHY

PrincipleWhy
FluidityThis game rewards fluidity on the trails, maintaining speed and flow is key. Avoid abrupt changes of direction, or excessive steering on straight sections of trail.
Airtime = Momentum ConservationJumping off small terrain features helps you carry speed over trail bumps. Airing over a section of trail is generally faster than rolling over it.
Flat > FloatHorizontal airtime preserves speed; too much vertical arc diminishes speed gains

JUMPING

TipEffect
Timing for Distance: Hop early, before the lip"Sucks up" the jump, creating more forward movement and distance with flatter arc
Timing for Airtime: Hop late, at the lipMaximizes vertical height and hang time for trick opportunities
Leaning forward slightly (LS forward) while hitting jumpHelps carry speed and avoid excessive vertical airtime
Slightly leaning forward once you are in the airSeems to help with speed and jump distance a bit
Pre-hop before entering the jumpSquashes rollers, prevents airtime, carries speed
RS from neutral/center → fully up"Half" bunnyhop (softer alternative to a full hop)
LS down while initially jumpingMay boost jump height/distance (unconfirmed—test yourself)
